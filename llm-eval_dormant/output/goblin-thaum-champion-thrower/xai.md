**Level 8 Goblin Thaumaturge (Weapon + Tome Implements) with Champion Dedication**

### Core Concept
A nimble goblin skirmisher who hurls daggers, returning javelins, and darts while using the **Tome implement** to grant himself and allies temporary floating +1/+2 status bonuses to AC, saves, and attacks via *Implement's Empowerment* and *Tome's Knowledge*. The Weapon implement is a **returning dagger** (or returning javelin) that benefits from *Exploit Vulnerability* and *Intensify Vulnerability*. Champion Dedication provides lay on hands for self-healing and the Champion's Reaction for defense while staying mobile with thrown weapons.

### Class: Thaumaturge
- **Key Ability:** Charisma
- **Implement 1 (Level 1):** Weapon Implement — **Dagger** (agile, finesse, thrown 10 ft, 1d4 piercing). Chosen because it is agile, has thrown trait, and works excellently with returning runes.
- **Implement 2 (Level 5):** **Tome** (Dark Archive). Excellent synergy with a throwing build because it provides floating status bonuses to attack rolls, AC, and saves without requiring actions in many cases, and *Tome's Knowledge* + *Implement's Empowerment* makes your thrown attacks much more accurate and damaging while also boosting your AC.

**Implement Synergy Explanation:**  
The Weapon implement gives you *Exploit Vulnerability* (extra damage + bonuses vs the target) and *Intensify Vulnerability* (stronger benefits). The Tome gives you *Tome's Knowledge* (status bonus to attacks/saves/AC that scales with level). This combination turns you into a highly mobile, hard-to-hit skirmisher who throws daggers/javelins with high accuracy and still has solid defenses.

### Backstory & Personality
**"Stabbin' Stix"** is a scrawny, bright-orange goblin who grew up in a junkyard on the outskirts of Absalom. After being chased out of too many taverns for "practicing knife tricks" on the patrons, he discovered an ancient thaumaturgic tome in a trash heap. The book started talking to him, and he decided the best way to make friends was to throw sharp things at bad guys from a safe distance while the book made him and his new "tallfolk" friends harder to hit. He now travels as a grinning, slightly unhinged skirmisher who cackles "Stix go zoom!" every time he hurls a returning dagger. He genuinely likes people — he just prefers them to be at least 30 feet away when he shows affection.
