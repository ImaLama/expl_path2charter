**Goblin Thaumaturge (Throwing Weapon Build) – Level 8**

### Core Concept
A highly mobile, chaotic goblin “artillerist” who uses a **Returning Rune** on thrown weapons as his Weapon Implement, paired with the **Mirror Implement** for exceptional defensive synergy. He leverages Champion Dedication (for Champion’s Reaction and Lay on Hands) to become surprisingly hard to kill while flinging daggers, shuriken, and spears from range.

---

### 1. Ancestry: Goblin
- **Heritage**: **Charhide Goblin** (Advanced Player’s Guide) – +2 circumstance bonus to saves vs fire, and you do not take persistent fire damage on a failure.
- **Ancestry Feats**:
  - Level 1: **Goblin Scuttle** (Core Rulebook) – excellent mobility when an ally moves next to you.
  - Level 5: **Very Sneaky** (Core Rulebook) – +2 circumstance to Stealth when you have cover or concealment; synergizes with goblin mobility and thrown-weapon hit-and-run tactics.

---

### 2. Background
**Street Urchin** (Core Rulebook)
- Trained in **Stealth**
- Trained in **Thievery**
- Background Feat: **Pickpocket** (free)

---

### 3. Class: Thaumaturge
**Key Implements**:
- **Weapon Implement**: Dagger (or Returning Shuriken if GM allows shuriken as a “weapon implement” – RAW a dagger is safest).  
  **Reason**: Daggers have the **Returning** rune applied at level 6+ (via Implement’s Empowerment). Thrown daggers with Returning is the cleanest, most reliable throwing weapon in the game.
- **Second Implement**: **Mirror Implement** (Thaumaturge, Player Core 2)  
  **Reason**: Mirror’s Reflection gives you a strong reaction that forces an enemy to reroll an attack against you or an ally within 30 ft (once per round). This stacks beautifully with the Champion’s Reaction (Glimpse of Redemption or Lay on Hands) and turns the goblin into a very annoying, hard-to-kill backline harasser.

**Implement Empowerment** at level 6 lets you put a +1 striking rune on the weapon implement for free, which is huge for thrown damage.

---

### 4. Champion Dedication & Archetype Feats
- **Level 2**: **Champion Dedication** (requires trained in Religion; see skills below). You must choose **Lawful Good** or **Neutral Good** alignment (most common is NG for a chaotic goblin). Grants:
  - Champion’s Reaction (Glimpse of Redemption is best for a ranged character)
  - Lay on Hands (1-action heal)
  - Divine Spellcasting (focus spells)

- **Level 4**: **Basic Champion Spellcasting** (Archetype feat) – required tax. Gives you the *champion* focus spells and lets you heighten Lay on Hands.

- **Level 6**: **Champion’s Reaction** improvement is already covered by the dedication, so we take a Thaumaturge feat here instead.

**Note on budget**: A *Returning* rune is 55 gp (level 3). A +1 potency rune on the implement is free via Implement’s Empowerment at level 6. A *Striking* rune is also free via the same feature. This keeps the build very budget-efficient.

### 9. Playstyle Summary & Synergy Explanation

- **Throwing loop**: Strike with Returning Dagger (or shuriken) → trigger Mirror’s Reflection or Champion’s Reaction when enemies attack you or allies → use Mirrored Aegis to give +1 AC to self + one ally.
- Dual-Implement Attack at level 8 lets you pop both implement benefits in one action and still Strike.
- Goblin Scuttle + Fleet + high Dexterity makes you extremely hard to pin down.
- Lay on Hands + high Charisma keeps the party alive while you stay at range.

**Uncertainties**:
- Whether your GM allows a shuriken as the Weapon Implement (RAW it must be a “weapon” you are proficient with; shuriken are martial, so you need martial weapon proficiency from somewhere — Champion Dedication does *not* give it). Dagger is 100% safe.
- Exact gold total can vary slightly depending on whether you start with a free returning rune or buy it.

### 10. Personality & Backstory

**“Stabby” Krikrik Sparkfang** is a small, soot-stained goblin with a permanent manic grin. Born in a junkyard on the edge of Absalom, he discovered an old, cracked hand-mirror that “talked back” when he threw knives at it. Convinced the mirror is a god (or at least a very opinionated friend), he now travels with a band of adventurers, hurling blades with gleeful abandon while the mirror yells insults at anything trying to hit him or his “big dumb friends.” He genuinely believes that if he throws enough sharp things, the universe will eventually run out of bad guys. He calls his favorite returning dagger “Mr. Stabby-Go-Home.”
