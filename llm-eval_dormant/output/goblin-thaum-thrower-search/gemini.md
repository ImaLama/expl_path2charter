Here is a complete, mechanically optimized Level 8 Goblin Thaumaturge build focused on throwing weapons, featuring the Champion Dedication. 

### **1. Ancestry Breakdown**
* **Ancestry**: Goblin
* **Heritage**: Unbreakable Goblin (Increases starting HP from 6 to 10, giving you the durability needed for a medium/close-range combatant).
* **Ancestry Feats**:
  * **Level 1**: **Goblin Song**. Gives you a strong 1-action ranged debuff (30 ft) that compares your Performance against their Will DC to give them a –1 penalty to Will and Perception.
  * **Level 5**: **Twitchy** (Level 1 feat). Grants a +1 circumstance bonus to AC and saves against hazards, and a +2 circumstance bonus to Initiative. Going first lets you set up your implements and Exploit Vulnerability immediately.

### **2. Background**
* **Background**: **Gladiator**
* **Skill Training**: Performance, Gladiatorial Lore.
* **Background Feat**: **Impressive Performance**. Allows you to use Performance instead of Diplomacy to Make an Impression, perfectly synergizing with your high Charisma and Goblin Song.

### **3. Class & Implements**
* **Class**: Thaumaturge
* **Key Ability**: Charisma
* **Implement 1 (Level 1)**: **Weapon (Trident)**. The Trident is a 1-handed martial melee weapon with the *Thrown 20 ft* trait and a 1d8 damage die. Because it is natively a 1-handed melee weapon, it legally qualifies as a Weapon Implement (unlike chakrams or boomerangs). You gain **Implement's Empowerment**, adding +2 damage per weapon damage die when holding it.
* **Implement 2 (Level 5)**: **Regalia**. The Regalia grants a passive aura giving you a +1 circumstance bonus to damage rolls and a +1 circumstance bonus to Charisma-based skills (Performance, Intimidation). This perfectly synergizes with your throwing damage, Goblin Song, and Demoralize. 
* **Implement Adept (Level 7)**: **Weapon**. Grants *Implement's Interruption*, giving you an Attack of Opportunity-style reaction if an enemy within your 5 ft melee reach uses a concentrate, manipulate, or move action. 

*Rules Note: Thaumaturges explicitly can use their Esoterica (like Exploit Vulnerability) even if their hands are full with two implements (Dark Archive, pg. 38). You can hold your Trident in one hand, Regalia in the other, and still function perfectly.*

### **4. Champion Dedication & Archetype**
* **Deity**: **Kurgess** (God of bravery and sports). Allows the Lawful Good alignment.
* **Cause**: **Paladin** (Requires Lawful Good). Grants the *Retributive Strike* reaction.
* **Archetype Feats**:
  * **Level 2**: Champion Dedication (Grants Heavy Armor proficiency, Religion, and Medicine).
  * **Level 4**: Basic Devotion -> **Ranged Reprisal**. Allows you to use Retributive Strike with a ranged weapon, or Step to put a foe in melee reach.
  * **Level 6**: **Champion's Reaction**. Grants the Paladin's *Retributive Strike*.
  * **Level 8**: Advanced Devotion -> **Healing Touch**. Grants the *Lay on Hands* devotion spell for a 1-action heal and +2 AC buff.

*Rules Note on Ranged Reprisal: The Thrown trait states a weapon "is a ranged weapon when thrown." By RAW, this allows you to trigger a Retributive Strike by throwing your Trident at an enemy who attacks an ally within 15 feet of you. If a strict GM disputes this, Ranged Reprisal still allows you to Step 5 feet and make a melee strike, which is incredibly potent.*

### **9. Personality & Backstory**
Once a scrappy pit-fighter in a rowdy gladiatorial arena, "Trident" (as the crowds called him) realized the cheers of the audience and the symbolism of his weapon carried actual, tangible magic. By leaning into the pageantry of the arena (his Regalia) and the esoteric weight of his chosen weapon, he tapped into the powers of a Thaumaturge. After a rigged match nearly cost an innocent rookie their life, Trident swore an oath to Kurgess—the god of sports, bravery, and fair play. Now a Paladin of the Strong Man, he travels the world, using his booming voice to rattle his enemies and throwing his magically returning trident to protect his allies from afar.
